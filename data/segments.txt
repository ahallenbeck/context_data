<p>Segments allow you to create logical groupings of your pages based on your needs, which you can then use to make your reporting more effective and to customize the auditing suite.</p>

<p>If you're new to segments, watch this short video to learn what segments are and how they can turbocharge the way you do SEO:</p>

<p>[intro music]</p>

<p>Websites today easily run into the thousands or even hundreds of thousands pages.</p>

<p>And let's face it, all pages are NOT created equal.</p>

<p>Your website has is main content pillars, it probably has a blog, and even some customers support content.</p>

<p>If you're in eCommerce, you've got product category and product detail pages too.</p>

<p>And then there are other qualities to consider, like page importance.</p>

<p>Pages getting the bulk of your traffic, or driving the most revenue, are much more important to you than others.</p>

<p>And this is where the ContentKing Segments come in!</p>

<p>They make it easy to cut through the static by grouping pages together, based on any page property you want.</p>

<p>Setting Segments up is a breeze and once done there are so many ways to work with them.</p>

<p>Use them for more focused reporting, set uzp custom alerts, and target your auditing on specific parts of the website.</p>
</div></details></figure></div>

<p>ContentKing comes with one default segment: <em>Important pages</em>. This segment includes the most important pages on the website based on ContentKing’s <a href="/support/page-importance/">Importance score</a>.</p>

<p>However, you can also create as many custom segments as you want, based on any filter criterion or any combination of different filter criteria.</p>

<h2 id="segments-are-dynamic">
<a class="anchor-link" aria-hidden="true" tabindex="-1" href="#segments-are-dynamic"></a>Segments are dynamic</h2>

<p>Segments are dynamic, which means that when pages start matching the underlying filter criteria they will automatically be placed in the segment. Conversely, pages that stop matching the filter criteria will be removed from the segment.</p>

<p>For example, let's say you create a segment for pages within the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">/blog/</code> URL directory called "<em>Blog articles</em>". Now whenever you publish a new blog article it will be automatically included in this segment.</p>

<h2 id="segment-suggestions">
<a class="anchor-link" aria-hidden="true" tabindex="-1" href="#segment-suggestions"></a>Segment suggestions</h2>

<p>You are completely free to segment on any criterion you want, but here are some examples to get you started:</p>

<ul>
  <li>
<strong>Indexable pages</strong><br>
Use the Indexable column to create a segment for all indexable pages on your website to ensure that there are no issues with indexing your content.</li>
  <li>
<strong>Main categories / topics</strong>
Perhaps a no-brainer, but creating segments around your main categories or topics is a great way to leverage the power of segments.</li>
  <li>
<strong>Recently added pages</strong><br>
Filter on "less than 7 days ago" in the <em>Discovered</em> and create a segment for this.</li>
  <li>
<strong>High performers</strong><br>
Connect with <a href="/support/google-analytics/">Google Analytics</a> or <a href="/support/google-search-console/">Google Search Console</a> and set up a segment for pages with <em>Top 5% Pageviews</em>.</li>
  <li>
<strong>Slow pages</strong><br>
Filter on "more than 600ms" in the <em>Download time</em> column.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="creating-segments">
<a class="anchor-link" aria-hidden="true" tabindex="-1" href="#creating-segments"></a>Creating segments</h2>

<p>Segments are created in the Segment editor, which can be accessed in multiple ways:</p>

<h3 id="accessing-the-segment-editor-from-the-pages-screen">
<a class="anchor-link" aria-hidden="true" tabindex="-1" href="#accessing-the-segment-editor-from-the-pages-screen"></a>Accessing the Segment editor from the Pages screen</h3>
<p>When filtering on any column on the <em>Pages</em> screen, you can jump directly to the Segment editor and create the segment with the filter criteria that you’re currently using:</p>

<section class="highlighted-box typo-block  highlighted-box--with-icon highlighted-box--steps-style "><div class="highlighted-box__content ">
<ol>
  <li>Set the filter criteria on the Pages screen according to your needs</li>
  <li>Open the blue <em>Active Filters toolbar</em> by hovering over it with your mouse</li>
  <li>Click on the <em>Create new segment</em> button</li>
</ol>
</div></section>

<h3 id="accessing-the-segment-editor-from-settings">
<a class="anchor-link" aria-hidden="true" tabindex="-1" href="#accessing-the-segment-editor-from-settings"></a>Accessing the Segment editor from Settings</h3>
<p>You can also access the Segment editor directly without having to configure any filters:</p>

<section class="highlighted-box typo-block  highlighted-box--with-icon highlighted-box--steps-style "><div class="highlighted-box__content ">
<ol>
  <li>Go to the <em>Segments</em> tab of the <em>Settings</em> section</li>
  <li>Click the <em>Create segment</em> button in the top-right corner of the Segments overview</li>
</ol>
</div></section>

<h3 id="creating-a-segment-in-the-segment-editor">
<a class="anchor-link" aria-hidden="true" tabindex="-1" href="#creating-a-segment-in-the-segment-editor"></a>Creating a segment in the Segment editor</h3>
<p>The process of creating a segment consists of 4 steps:</p>

<section class="highlighted-box typo-block  highlighted-box--with-icon highlighted-box--steps-style "><div class="highlighted-box__content ">
<ol>
  <li>Setting a label</li>
  <li>Setting filter criteria</li>
  <li>Setting a size limit</li>
  <li>Saving the segment</li>
</ol>
</div></section>

<p><strong>Note:</strong> a segment can be created without a size limit (3rd step) in case filter criteria are set, or without filter criteria (2nd step) in case a size limit is set.</p>

<p>The individual steps are described below.</p>

<h3 id="setting-a-label">
<a class="anchor-link" aria-hidden="true" tabindex="-1" href="#setting-a-label"></a>Setting a label</h3>
<p>To set a label for a segment, follow these steps:</p>

<section class="highlighted-box typo-block  highlighted-box--with-icon highlighted-box--steps-style "><div class="highlighted-box__content ">
<ol>
  <li>Click the <em>Set label</em> button in the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Label</code> box on the right side of the Segment editor</li>
  <li>Set the color of the segment</li>
  <li>Set the short name (an icon or two characters) and the full name of the segment</li>
  <li>Confirm the label configuration by clicking the <em>Set label</em> button</li>
</ol>

</div></section>

<h3 id="setting-filter-criteria">
<a class="anchor-link" aria-hidden="true" tabindex="-1" href="#setting-filter-criteria"></a>Setting filter criteria</h3>
<p>To set a filter criterion for a segment, follow these steps:</p>

<section class="highlighted-box typo-block  highlighted-box--with-icon highlighted-box--steps-style "><div class="highlighted-box__content ">
<ol>
  <li>Click the <em>Add criteria</em> button in the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Filter Criteria</code> box on the right side of the Segment editor</li>
  <li>Choose the column which you want to use for filtering and add it to the screen by clicking the <em>Add this column</em> button</li>
  <li>Set the filter value in the column according to your needs</li>
</ol>
</div></section>

<p>To add more filter criteria, easily add more columns by repeating the above-described process.</p>

<p>To remove a filter criterion from the segment definition, click the cross icon in the top-right corner of the given column.</p>

<h3 id="setting-a-size-limit">
<a class="anchor-link" aria-hidden="true" tabindex="-1" href="#setting-a-size-limit"></a>Setting a size limit</h3>
<p>Limiting the segment size will set the maximum number of pages that the segment will contain. To set a size limit for a segment, follow these steps:</p>

<section class="highlighted-box typo-block  highlighted-box--with-icon highlighted-box--steps-style "><div class="highlighted-box__content ">
<ol>
  <li>Click the <em>Set limit</em> button in the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Size Limit</code> box on the right side of the Segment editor</li>
  <li>Choose the column based on which the pages will be sorted</li>
  <li>Set the sorting order: <em>Descending</em> or <em>Ascending</em>
</li>
  <li>Set the size limit: <em>Percentage of pages</em> (1–99%) or <em>Number of pages</em> (an absolute number greater than 0)</li>
  <li>Confirm the size limit configuration by clicking the <em>Set size limit</em> button</li>
</ol>

<h3 id="saving-the-segment">
<a class="anchor-link" aria-hidden="true" tabindex="-1" href="#saving-the-segment"></a>Saving the segment</h3>
<p>Once you have set the label, the filter criteria and/or the size limit for the segment, you can save the segment by clicking the <em>Save segment</em> button in the bottom-right corner of the Segment editor.</p>

<h3 id="creating-a-static-segment">
<a class="anchor-link" aria-hidden="true" tabindex="-1" href="#creating-a-static-segment"></a>Creating a Static segment</h3>

<p>In most situations, the filters that ContentKing has out of the box are sufficient and a preferred way to segment your pages.</p>

<p>However, if you want to group certain pages together that can't be grouped with the filters, you can create a <strong>Static segment!</strong></p>

<p>Static segments should only be used as a last resort, as opposed to dynamic segments, they don't update automatically, so they can't be used for alerting.</p>

<p>You can create a static segment in the following way:</p>

<section class="highlighted-box typo-block  highlighted-box--with-icon highlighted-box--steps-style "><div class="highlighted-box__content ">
<ol>
  <li>Go to the <em>Segments</em> tab of the <em>Settings</em> section</li>
  <li>
    <p>Click the down arrow next to <em>Create segment</em> button and choose <em>Static segment</em> in the top-right corner of the Segments overview</p>

    <div class="media-container " style="max-width: 2438px;"><img loading="lazy" class="image" src="/media/support/contentking-segments-static-arrow@2x.png" height="1082" width="2438" alt="Screenshot showing a window in which you can specify to create a static segment" title="Choosing a static segment option in the segment section"></div>
  </li>
  <li>Enter the URLs that you want to group into the URL box</li>
  <li>
    <p>Set a label for the segment and hit the <em>Save segment</em> button</p>

    <div class="media-container " style="max-width: 3420px;"><img loading="lazy" class="image" src="/media/support/contentking-segments-static-segment@2x.png" height="1990" width="3420" alt="Screenshot showing a window of the static segment editor" title="The view of a static segment editor"></div>
  </li>
</ol>

</div></section>

<h2 id="working-with-segments-in-contentking">
<a class="anchor-link" aria-hidden="true" tabindex="-1" href="#working-with-segments-in-contentking"></a>Working with segments in ContentKing</h2>
<p>There are multiple places across our platform where you can use the segments that you’ve created.</p>

<h3 id="using-segments-for-filtering-on-the-pages-screen">
<a class="anchor-link" aria-hidden="true" tabindex="-1" href="#using-segments-for-filtering-on-the-pages-screen"></a>Using segments for filtering on the Pages screen</h3>

<p>On the Pages screen, you can use the <em>Segment</em> column to filter on a specific segment, and even combine multiple segments in the filter:</p>

<section class="highlighted-box typo-block  highlighted-box--with-icon highlighted-box--steps-style "><div class="highlighted-box__content ">
<ol>
  <li>Open the <em>Segments</em> filter</li>
  <li>Drag and drop the newly created segment from the <em>Segments</em> container to the <em>Included in</em> container</li>
  <li>Do the same with the default <em>Important pages</em> segment</li>
</ol>
</div></section>

<p>Now you will only see pages that are in both segments.</p>

<p>Want to see pages that are in either one segment? Then toggle the <em>AND/OR</em> button to <em>OR</em>.</p>

<p>Or what about pages that are in one segment but NOT in the other? Simple: just drag the segment you want to exclude to the <em>And not included in</em> container.</p>

<h3 id="using-segments-on-the-dashboard--in-the-contentking-weekly-report">

<p>The Dashboard screen in ContentKing shows you the most important data for all scopes that are defined on the website.</p>

<p>You can configure which scopes will be shown on the dashboard using the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Scope</code> dropdown.</p>

<p>The scopes configuration from the dashboard also applies to the scopes included in the Weekly Report. You can read more about this <a href="/support/weekly-reports/#selecting-the-scopes-to-be-included-in-the-weekly-report">here</a>.</p>

<h3 id="using-segments-in-issues">
<a class="anchor-link" aria-hidden="true" tabindex="-1" href="#using-segments-in-issues"></a>Using segments in Issues</h3>
<p>There are two ways how you can use segments in the Issues section in ContentKing:</p>

<h4 id="filter-out-issues-for-a-specific-segment">Filter out issues for a specific segment</h4>
<p>The <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Segments</code> filter in the <em>Issues</em> section allows you to view the issue data for a specific segment.</p>

<p>So for example, if you have created a segment for your most visited pages, you can easily view the most pressing issues detected in this segment using the <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">Segments</code> filter.</p>

<h4 id="ignore-issues-in-specific-segments">Ignore issues in specific segments</h4>
<p>ContentKing also allows you to ignore issues in specific segments to tailor the auditing suite to your specific needs. You can read more about this <a href="/support/issues/#issue-ignoring">here</a>.</p>

<h3 id="using-segments-together-with-alerts">
<a class="anchor-link" aria-hidden="true" tabindex="-1" href="#using-segments-together-with-alerts"></a>Using segments together with Alerts</h3>
<p>In ContentKing you can create different alert definitions for different segments of your website with different configurations.</p>

<p>For example, you can set up alert definitions with the highest Sensitivity level for the most important segments on your website to make sure you are alerted about every single change in these segments. You can read more about this <a href="/support/configuring-alerts/#higher-sensitivity-for-important-segments">here</a>.</p>

<p>On top of that, you can also set up alerts for pages entering or leaving a segment. This feature is very powerful as it offers a wide range of configuration possibilities, essentially allowing you to be alerted about any element on your website that is important for you.</p>

<p>You can read more about this <a href="/support/configuring-alerts/#pages-entering-leaving-segment">here</a>.</p>

<h3 id="editing-segments">
<a class="anchor-link" aria-hidden="true" tabindex="-1" href="#editing-segments"></a>Editing segments</h3>

<p>You can edit your existing segments at any time. To edit a segment, follow these steps:</p>

<section class="highlighted-box typo-block  highlighted-box--with-icon highlighted-box--steps-style "><div class="highlighted-box__content ">
<ol>
  <li>In the <em>Segments</em> overview click the settings icon next to the segment you want to edit.</li>
  <li>Edit the label, filter criteria and/or size limit according to your needs (as described <a href="/support/segments/#creating-a-segment-in-the-segment-editor">above</a>)</li>
  <li>Once done, simply click the <em>Save changes</em> button.</li>
</ol>
</div></section>

<h3 id="deleting-segments">
<a class="anchor-link" aria-hidden="true" tabindex="-1" href="#deleting-segments"></a>Deleting segments</h3>

<p>You can also delete any segment that you don’t need anymore. To delete a segment, follow these steps:</p>

<section class="highlighted-box typo-block  highlighted-box--with-icon highlighted-box--steps-style "><div class="highlighted-box__content ">
<ol>
  <li>In the <em>Segments</em> overview click the trash bin icon next to the segment you want to delete.</li>
  <li>In the window that appears confirm the deletion by typing <code class="language-plaintext highlighter-rouge">DELETE</code> and click <em>Delete segment</em>.</li>
</ol>
</div></section>

<h3 id="importing-segments">
<a class="anchor-link" aria-hidden="true" tabindex="-1" href="#importing-segments"></a>Importing segments</h3>

<p>ContentKing also allows you to import segments from one website to another.</p>

<p>Thanks to this option you don’t need to create segments from scratch on each website, but you can simply import your existing segments from one website to another.</p>

<p>To import segments to a website, follow these steps:</p>

<section class="highlighted-box typo-block  highlighted-box--with-icon highlighted-box--steps-style "><div class="highlighted-box__content ">
<ol>
  <li>In the <em>Segments</em> overview click the <em>Import segments</em> button in the top-right corner.</li>
  <li>In the window that appears, select from which website you want to import segments and click <em>Continue to segment selection</em>.</li>
  <li>Select the segments that you want to import.</li>
  <li>Confirm the selection by clicking the <em>Import segments</em> button.</li>
</ol>

<p>When you use our website <a href="https://www.contentkingapp.com/">www.contentkingapp.com</a> (“Website”), we may place cookies. This Cookie Policy explains what cookies are and in which way cookies are stored on, and information is read from, your computer, mobile device and/or tablet (“Devices”). Please read this Cookie Policy carefully in order to understand what type of cookies ContentKing uses, the information we collect using cookies and how that information is used.</p>

<h2 id="what-are-cookies">What are cookies?</h2>

<p>Cookies are small text files which are stored on the browser or hard drive of your Device when you visit a webpage or use an application. Cookies may be needed to show the webpage or application on your Device and are also used to enhance the user experience. Cookies cannot damage your Devices or the files saved on it. There are different types of cookies. Some cookies come directly from our website (first party cookies) and others come from third parties which place cookies on our site (third party cookies).</p>

<p>Cookies can be stored for varying lengths of time on your browser or device. Session cookies are deleted from your computer or device when you close your web-browser. Persistent cookies will remain stored on your computer or Device until deleted or until they reach their expiry date.</p>

<h2 id="how-do-we-use-cookies">How do we use cookies?</h2>

<h3 id="essential-technical-cookies">Essential (Technical) cookies</h3>

<p>We try to provide an advanced and user-friendly website that adapts automatically to needs and wishes of our visitors and users. To achieve this, we use technical cookies to i.e. show you our Website, to make it function correctly, to create your ContentKing account, to sign you in and to manage your requests. These technical cookies are necessary for our website to function properly.</p>

<h3 id="functional-cookies">Functional cookies</h3>

<p>We also use functional cookies to remember your preferences and settings (as username, password, language etc.) and to help you to use the Website efficiently and effectively. These functional cookies are not strictly necessary for the functioning of our Website, but they add functionality for you and enhance your experience.</p>

<h3 id="analytical-cookies">Analytical cookies</h3>

<p>We use analytical cookies in order to collect statistics about the use and visits of the Website. These analytic cookies generate statistical and other information about website use by means of cookies, which are stored on users’ Devices. The information generated relating to our Website is used to create reports about the use of the Website. Analytical cookies may be placed without your consent, unless such cookies have an impact on your privacy. In such cases, prior consent will be asked for.</p>

<h3 id="google-analytics">Google Analytics</h3>

<p>We use Google Analytics cookies in order to collect statistics about the use and visits of the Website. With these analytical cookies a permanent cookie is saved on your device to register your use of the Website. Google Analytics analyses this data and provides us with the results. This way, we obtain insight into the traffic of the Website and the way in which the Website is used. Based on this information we are able to make specific adjustments to the Website.</p>

<p>The information that we obtain is transferred to Google and stored by Google on servers outside the European Economic Area. We have entered into a data processing agreement with Google in which agreements have been made regarding the processing of personal data.</p>

<p>If you would like more information about analytical cookies and their expiry date, please visit the Google Analytics <a href="https://developers.google.com/analytics/devguides/collection/gajs/cookie-usage">information page</a>. Google also offers an <a href="https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout?hl=en">opt-out option</a> for data collection within the scope of Google Analytics.</p>

<h3 id="marketing-cookies">Marketing cookies</h3>

<p>We use third party marketing cookies in order to follow your internet browsing behavior and collect data and information on your browsing behavior from various websites you visited. This information is used to make the content of the displayed advertisements as relevant as possible, and to limit repetition of the same advertisements.</p>

<h3 id="social-plug-ins">Social plug-ins</h3>

<p>By using the Website you can get access to social media websites, such as Facebook, Twitter and LinkedIn. These buttons come with a code which has been made by these networks themselves. Using these so called social plug-ins you can login to your social media profile and subsequently share information from the Website with others. With these social plug-ins third party marketing cookies are saved on your Devices. These cookies serve the purpose of optimizing your user experience.</p>

<p>Companies such as LinkedIn, Twitter and Facebook may share your personal data outside the European Economic Area. Please read our Privacy Policy <a href="/legal/privacy-policy/">here</a> and the respective social network’s privacy statement to see how these companies treat your (personal) data.</p>

<h3 id="pixels">Pixels</h3>

<p>We also place pixels of third parties. A pixel keeps track of your surfing behavior and how you use the Website. These data are aggregated and give us information about the target group that visits our website. Based on this information, we can show an advertisement to a specific or similar target group on the website of the third party that produced the pixel.</p>

<h2 id="disable-or-delete-cookies">Disable or delete cookies</h2>

<p>When you first visit the Website, we ask you to give consent for cookies that are not necessary or functional and that have an impact on your privacy. If you do not give consent, these cookies will not be placed. In that case, you may not be able to use all the functionalities on our Website.</p>

<p>You can configure your browser so that you do not receive any cookies the next time you use the website. You can read here how you can remove different types of cookies in different browsers. Please consult the help-function of your browser if your browser is not listed below.</p>

<ul>
  <li><a href="https://support.google.com/chrome/answer/95647?co=GENIE.Platform%3DDesktop&amp;hl=en-GB">Chrome</a></li>
  <li><a href="https://support.mozilla.org/en-US/kb/clear-cookies-and-site-data-firefox">Firefox</a></li>

<p>Note: Refusing or deleting cookies only affects the Device and the browser on which you perform this action. If you use different Devices and/or browsers you will need to repeat the above described actions on these Devices and/or browsers.</p>
